INTEGRATED COMPANY POLICY – SUMMARY

PANATTA in the process of designing and building professional fitness machines for several market targets, commits to apply the company philosophy based on the below principles:

  • ETHICS
    Commitment to take a fair and loyal approach towards all the market stake-holders whether they are partners, clients, providers, institutions and communities:
    Actually, Panatta ensures:
    • The launch to the market of safe products and permanent post sales service;
    • The enhancement, in terms of efficiency and effectiveness, of its own Management Systems- for Quality – Environment – Safety in accordance with the current national and international law.
  • PASSION FOR EXCELLENCE
    Intended as aesthetical and functional products care and scientific research focused on innovation;
  • ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION
    that happens through constant improvement of the environmental performances for:
    • minimizing, when possible, any negative impact on the environment that might be caused by the activities and necessary processes for the product life cycle;
    • meet the expectations on the environmental improvement of the involved Parties:

Panatta wants to be a flexible, efficient and reliable partner for each one of the Clients and for any other external or internal party involved. The company aims to detect the correct actions and strategies in order to ensure:

  • Complete satisfaction about the product features and included services (obligation for Client, Company);
  • Meet the environmental requirements demanded (obligation for the involved Parties and the Company);
  • Management of risks and opportunities about actions supported by:
    • Quality- Environment integrated policy;
    • Annual Quality objectives and Annual Environmental objectives;
    • Results of the inspections;
    • Data analysis concerning:
      • Customers satisfaction;
      • Environmental performances;
      • Compliance with the requirements of the product;
      • Compliance with the requirements of the Environment Management;
      • Features and performance of the processes and products;
      • Supplier’s performance
    • Corrective actions and suggestions for improvement.
